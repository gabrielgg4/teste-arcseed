RPs(render points)

Render Points are the resource used to render the images on the platform, each tool uses a different ammount of resources. You can see the ammount you have left of Render points on the top right of your screen.

You don't need to memorize anything, the ammount used per render will be displayed on the render button as you can see on the image below, but there are colors corresponding to each ammount used so it is easier to remember, the colors being:

  • USES 1 RP
  • USES 2 RP
  • USES 3 RP
  • USES 4 RP
  • USES 5 RP

    Precision

    There are two precision levels to render with, the Standard and the Professional.

    • Standard - Requires less RPs to render but generates smaller and a little less precise images.
    • Professional - Requires more RPs to render and generate bigger images with more details.

    OBS : Keep in mind that the enhance in quality from standard to professional may vary, since the AI is trying to create a new image on top of the other it may change a few details of it.

    • Fine-Tuning

    Fine-tunes are the most advanced tools to modify your rendering, the can perform minimal changes to enhance the image or completely change it, each tool has a different set of fine-tuning options.
    You don't need to change any of these settings to reach good quality images, but they may improve a lot the final render if you know what you are doing.
    The sketch 2 Render has different sets of sliders if you choose Interiors or Exteriors:

  • AI Imagination : The AI Imagination fine-tune adjusts the attention of the AI to your choices, higher values will make the AI be more focused while lower values will let the AI wonder more.
    Keep in mind that low values can be foggy while higher values can look more contrasting. It appears on the exterior and interior tools.

    In the end is a matter of choice and testing, there is no right answer here.
  • Shape Strenght : The Shape Strenght represents how much the AI will be attentive to the shapes of the input image(the image you uploaded).It appears only on the interiors.

    You can chose three values to it
    • Creative : Gives more freedom to the AI to imagine, it tends to follow slightly less of the input image.
    • Balanced : Balances creativity but tries to keep the shapes correct.
    • Precise : Tries to follow the shapes without being much creative.

    OBS : keep in mind that the fine-tune sliders will influentiate one another, The finetune exposure is closely related to the strenght, because of that, for better results try to combine both.

  • Shape Exposure : The Shape Exposure represents for how long the AI will try to keep the Shape Strenght. This one is a bit tricky to understand, but think this way, the Shape Strenght controls how strong the shapes will be held during the render and the Shape Exposure is how much of the render time the AI will be holding this shape. It appears on the exterior and interior tools.

    Ex : if the render time is one minute and we have three options, soft, moderate and strong, the soft will keep the shape strenght running for 25% of the render, the moderate 50% and the strong 80%, this means that you can combine a higher strenght with a low exposure if your results are not getting as you need, or vice-versa.
    There are three values to choose:
    • Soft : Runs the Shape Strenght for 25% of the total render time.
    • Moderate : Runs the Shape Strenght for 50% of the render time.
    • Strong : Runs the Shape Strenght for 80% of the render time.

    OBS : We don't use 100% of exposure because our model does not showed increased results with it, but soon you will be able to change these values more freely on the Pro editor.

  • Input Depth : The input depth takes the depth information of the image and use it to control the shape of objects on the scene. It appears only on the Exterior tool.

    You can chose three values to it
    • Neutral : Do not calculate the depth information of the image.
    • Shalow : Uses 30% of the depth information on the render.
    • Balanced : Uses 60% of the depth information on the render.
    • Deep : Uses all the depth information on the render.
  • Contour Strenght : The countour strenght traces lines around the corners of the objects on the image, these lines then are taken as guides to create the render, the stronguer this is, more it will follow. It appears only on the Exterior tool.

    You can chose three values to it

  • AI Imagination : The AI Imagination fine-tune adjusts the attention of the AI to your choices, higher values will make the AI be more focused while lower values will let the AI wonder more.
    Keep in mind that low values can be foggy while higher values can look more contrasting. It appears on the simple and advanced tools.

    In the end is a matter of choice and testing, there is no right answer here.
  • Shape Strenght : The Shape Strenght represents how much the AI will be attentive to the shapes of the input image(the image you uploaded).It appears only on the Advanced tools.

    You can chose three values to it
    • Creative : Gives more freedom to the AI to imagine, it tends to follow slightly less of the input image.
    • Balanced : Balances creativity but tries to keep the shapes correct.
    • Precise : Tries to follow the shapes without being much creative.

    OBS : keep in mind that the fine-tune sliders will influentiate one another, The finetune exposure is closely related to the strenght, because of that, for better results try to combine both.

  • Shape Exposure : The Shape Exposure represents for how long the AI will try to keep the Shape Strenght. This one is a bit tricky to understand, but think this way, the Shape Strenght controls how strong the shapes will be held during the render and the Shape Exposure is how much of the render time the AI will be holding this shape. It appears only in advanced tools. The changes in results are mild in this case, but they can help fixing minor issues.

    Ex : if the render time is one minute and we have three options, soft, moderate and strong, the soft will keep the shape strenght running for 25% of the render, the moderate 50% and the strong 80%, this means that you can combine a higher strenght with a low exposure if your results are not getting as you need, or vice-versa.
    There are three values to choose:
    • Soft : Runs the Shape Strenght for 25% of the total render time.
    • Moderate : Runs the Shape Strenght for 50% of the render time.
    • Strong : Runs the Shape Strenght for 80% of the render time.

    OBS : We don't use 100% of exposure because our model does not showed increased results with it, but soon you will be able to change these values more freely on the Pro editor.

  • Style Transfer : The Style transfer represents how much of the style the AI will use while generating the image. Strong values will bring more of the original style to the render while weak values will soft the stylization. It appears on the simple and advanced tools.

    On the gif below you can see the influence of the Style Transfer from weak to Strong.

  • Style Exposure : This value represents for how long the style transfer will last, weaker values will take only a small part of the rendering process to change the style of the final image while stronger values will use the whole rendering process to change the style. It appears on the simple and advanced tools.

    Ex : if the render time is one minute the weak will keep the stylizing process running for 20% of the render and the strong for 100%.
  • Originality : The Originality represents how much of the input image to receive the style will change on the rendering process. It appears only on the advanced tools.

    If the value is the minimum the image won't change, if it is on the highest it will change completely. On the Gif below you can see how low values barely change the image while with higher values the render absorbs completely the style.
